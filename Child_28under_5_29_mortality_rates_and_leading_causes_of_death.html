Child (under 5) Mortality rates and Leading causes of Death : A case study¶

We note the steady decline in mortality rates of children under the age of 5 in the past twenty-five years.¶
This is based on data collected from UNICEF and WHO.¶
Also the leading causes for death among children under the age of 5 is noted.¶
As a case study we consider the incidence of high mortality rates in children under 5 years for three leading causes : Malaria, Pneumonia and Diarrhoea .¶

The supporting graphs show the countries where under 5 mortality rates are the highest for these causes.¶

Under 5 mortality date 1990-2016¶

The global under-five mortality rate declined by 56 per cent, from 93 deaths per 1,000 live births in 1990 to 41 per 1,000 in 2016. Despite substantial progress, improving child survival remains a matter of urgent concern.¶

In 2016, an estimated 5.6 million children died before reaching their fifth birthday, mostly from preventable diseases. This translates to 15,000 under-five deaths per day, an intolerably high number of largely preventable child deaths.¶
